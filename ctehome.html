What is CTE?
CTE stands for Career and Technical Education, and our school integrates CTE into its course curriculums. BRHS offers several courses for students to pursue CTE, such as Photographic Technology. Additionally, each CTE themed course offers industrial certifications that give students a head-start in their pursuit of technical careers.
Collaboration
Hands-On Teamwork
In modern technical careers, employees are expected to collaborate and work is much less individualized than ever before. As a result, BRHS seeks to prepare students for this adjustment in their future careers through CTE. In extracurricular CTE activities such as TSA and FBLA, students collaborate in team projects that they present to judges as a team. Through these projects, students learn the values of teamwork, responsibility, and leadership under the guiding hand of their advisors.
Communication
Social Development
One of the main concepts stressed by CTE is that in order to progress in an event, students must learn to collaborate efficiently and professionally, fostering a healthy environment for each student to work in. Common workplace factors such as professionalism, deadline responsibility, team management, and workplace collaboration apply to CTE and extracurricular activities based in CTE.
Preparation
Career Readiness
Often, students miss out on a healthy exposure to careers while in high school. Through CTE, students can gain basic experience in the qualities required for different technical careers. Apart from real job experience, CTE is usually the primary and only method for high-school students to gain necessary career experience in preparation
