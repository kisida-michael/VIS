The Optimal Curriculum
CTE Courses
CTE stands for Career and Technical Education, and our school integrates CTE into its course curriculums. BRHS offers several courses for students to pursue CTE, such as Photographic Technology. Additionally, each CTE themed course offers industrial certifications that give students a head-start in their pursuit of technical careers.
Teachers
At Braden River High School, our CTE teachers make it their goal to prepare their students for future careers. Utilizing student-friendly training courses, BRHS CTE teachers ensure that their students are prepared to pass certifications pertaining to their area of study.

Engineering Courses
Applied Engineering Technology teaches the fundamentals of engineering with the engineering design process, industry level certifications, and hands-on projects that promote teamwork. Students learn how to appropriately analyze a problem and research into different solutions before developing an actual prototype. These steps then introduce the students on how real engineers approach an obstacle, and how they begin to find solutions.
CAD
Students develop skills in Computer Aided Design through certification training in CAD software such as SolidWorks, Revit, and Inventor. Through assistive training courses such as SolidProfessor and the guidance of Engineering teachers Mrs. Hudson and Mr. Patula, students can certify in these programs and will be prepared to demonstrate their CAD skills in future careers.
Construction
Students develop skills in construction through woodworking projects assigned by Mrs. Hudson and Mr. Patula. These projects range from building picnic tables and wood puzzles, involving the use of several pieces of industrial equipment which students can learn how to use.
Drafting
Students develop skills in drafting through CAD programs such as Revit and Solidworks, which involve the creation of technical drawings. Using these programs, students will apply their knowledge of drafting to develop designs for several engineering tasks, which can range from drafting blueprints for puzzle cubes to picnic tables.
Robotics Courses
Applied Robotics provides students with an introduction to robotics and drone technology. In the course, students will develop skills in application development through the VEX Robotics competition and will earn certifications in programs such as SolidWorks and a commercial drone license.
Design
Using the engineering design process, students develop designs based in VEX Robotics using processes common in the modern engineering field. Members utilize both hand-drawn and computer-aided drawings for VEX Robotics, providing a blueprint to outline necessary components. Additionally, teams of students will develop workplace values and ethics through collaborating in the design process.
Programming
Students can develop skills in programming languages such as ROBOTC when utilizing VEX Robotics. The Applied Robotics elective offers an introductory overview of programming, outlining the basis for languages such as ROBOTC to engage students’ problem solving and critical thinking skills.
Building
In the building process, students combine knowledge from design and programming to create a finished product. This step involves students’ creativity, cooperation, intuition, and attention to detail. Through the building process, students combine several engineering skills and concepts, preparing for testing and the final product.
Digital Photography
Digital Photography provides the basics and essentials for photography careers. Students will learn basic camera operations, lighting, marketing, and graphic design. Adobe certifications are offered, including Photoshop. For students interested in digital design and photography, Digital Photography is the recommended course.
Photography
Students investigate the basics of photography and the concepts behind making digital media through techniques such as the Rule of Thirds. Students take different types of photos including candids, portraits, landscapes. They learn camera functions, including adjusting aperture, shutter speed, and exposure.
Editing
Students in Digital Photography class are trained in a variety of Adobe programs for editing, including Lightroom, Photoshop, Premiere, etc. Students learn both graphic design principles and how to interact with editing softwares and forms of multimedia.
Marketing
Students learn digital editing techniques that are commonly applied in the commercial world. These skills include photo retouching, exposure balance, and additive visual effects. There is an emphasis placed on professionalism and media specific to advertising.
Advanced Placement Courses
Braden River High School provides its students with a chance to challenge themselves through a number of AP courses. These rigorous college-level classes teach students valuable knowledge and enable them to stand out from the crowd when applying for college.
AP Computer Science Principles
This course is designed for students who are strong in mathematics and analytical thinking. The course is an introduction to programming and will instruct students in the programming language JavaScript, with an emphasis on writing apps, games, and other tasks. Within the course, seven core topics are discussed: The Internet, Programming, Algorithms, Data & Information, Abstraction, Creativity, and Global Impacts.
AP Capstone Seminar
This course focuses heavily on the research skills needed to formally document and present findings on a specific issue. Students learn to formulate questions that are relevant, open-ended, and address multiple perspectives. Students then analyze and research these questions, utilizing skills such as source analysis and formal citation to create a research base for their claims. Next, students synthesize this information to develop well-reasoned arguments, ending their studies by presenting their information via a multimedia presentation. Students complete this process as a team in the first semester, and individually in the second semester.
AP Calculus AB/BC
After completing Precalculus, students can take the AP Calculus path, beginning with Calc AB (Calc 1) and ending with Calc BC (Calc 2). These courses explore concepts such as differentiation, integration, motion of bodies, and applications of derivatives and integrals.
AP Human Geography
Designed for ambitious incoming freshmen, AP Human Geography focuses on the processes that have shaped human understanding of the natural world and its geography. Key topics discussed in the course are culture, language, ethnicity, religion, agriculture, and politics. Students will also develop critical thinking and argumentative skills in the course.
AP World History
This course focuses on the human interactions and processes that have shaped global history. Broken into several periods spanning from 8000 B.C.E to the present, AP World History explores themes such as social interactions, political history, economics, and culture throughout history. Additionally, students will develop skills employed by historians.
AP United States History
Students in this course will investigate the developments that shaped the United States from 1491 to the present. The course offers seven historical themes, including National Identity, Politics, Technology/Economics, Culture/Society, Migration/Settlement, Geography, and Global Influence. Students will also develop skills utilized by historians.
AP European History
Broken into four major periods, students will analyze the processes and developments centered around Europe from 1450 to the present. The course offers six themes, spanning from European global influence to statesmanship and national identity. Students will also develop historical thinking skills.
AP United States Government & Politics
The AP US GOV course offers a college-level, nonpartisan perspective on the political culture of the United States, as well as relationships between political institutions, processes, and behaviors. In addition to the primary course material, students will also complete an independently researched political science project.
AP Macroeconomics
Students will learn the principles of economics that apply to an economic system as a whole. The course aims to familiarize students with the financial sector as well as economic stabilization policies, international business, and growth. The course is equivalent to a one-semester college introductory course.
AP Environmental Science
APES is a rigorous course providing students with concepts regarding natural and man-made environmental issues, solutions to these issues, and scientific principles through field investigation. The course is equivalent to a one-semester introductory environmental science course in college.
AP Biology
The course offers core theories governing biological systems, processes, and living organisms. AP Biology is centered around four main themes, which deal with concepts of evolution, free energy, responses to stimuli, and biological interaction. Students are expected to develop testable predictions, hypotheses, and explanations of biological phenomena.
AP Physics
Students will investigate Newtonian mechanics, electric circuits, and other mechanical/physical concepts. The course is algebra-based and is equivalent to a single-semester course in college.
AP Chemistry
The course involves the exploration of six big ideas, including atomic arrangement, thermodynamics, transfer of electrons, chemical properties, and physical properties. AP Chemistry is heavily involved in mathematics, scientific inquiry, and the use of representations and models. Additionally, AP Chemistry focuses on data analysis in coordination with the formation of scientific theories.
AP English Language & Composition
This course focuses on the analytical skills needed to evaluate argumentative texts and the rhetorical skills needed to create arguments. Equivalent to a two-semester introductory college course in rhetoric and composition, AP Language prepares students to write with effective persuasive technique and maturity.
AP English Literature & Composition
Students in this course will develop critical analysis skills when deconstructing literature. Equivalent to a two-semester introductory college course in literary analysis, this course includes intensive study of literature across a wide range of time periods and genres.
AP Art History
Equivalent to a two-semester college course, AP Art History focuses on the understanding of complex historical contexts of various forms of media. Students in the course will examine different artistic styles from various cultures and historical periods.
AP Music Theory
This course develops a student’s ability to comprehend and describe elements of music presented in a score. Equivalent to a one or two-semester introductory college course, AP Music Theory aims to present various compositional skills and methods for score analysis.
